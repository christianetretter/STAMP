Restaurant Recommendations

Hopefully you are looking forward to the conference dinner at Restaurant Rosengarten on Wednesday. Until then you are probably wondering where might be a good place to eat in Bern. Here are our recommendations, which might be more locally sourced than the broad tourist information. But be warned that the Swiss restaurants close up their kitchens earlier than you might be used to, often around 9pm. Below you also find the opening hours to each restaurant, however, we recommend checking the websites for yourself, as changes might occur.

Café du Commerce

A historic culinary landmark in Bern's old town with roots dating back to the Middle Ages. The building at Gerechtigkeitsgasse 74 has served as a bakery in the 15th century, then a soup kitchen, before becoming Café Italien and finally Restaurant Commerce. Since 1947, when Juan Augé installed the first kitchen, it has been serving authentic Spanish cuisine. The restaurant gained fame as a cultural hub where artists, politicians, and locals mingled, hosting figures like Meret Oppenheim, Louis Armstrong, and Samuel Beckett. Today, under the leadership of Rui and Anabela Pacheco, it continues its tradition of excellent Iberian cuisine in an atmosphere that bridges the gap between bohemian and bourgeois.

Address: Gerechtigkeitsgasse 74, 3011 Bern

Cuisine: Spanish, Portuguese

Opening Hours: Tue-Sat 10:00-14:30, 17:30-23:30, Mon & Sun Closed

Special Features: Historic Location, Traditional Atmosphere, Iberian Specialties

Huusbüffe

A family-friendly self-service restaurant located on top of the Gurten mountain, offering panoramic views of Bern and the Aare River. The restaurant features various buffets with a wide selection of salads, hot dishes, and desserts, including a special children's buffet. With its spacious terraces and adjacent play area, it's perfect for families and groups. The restaurant is divided into different areas by partitions, creating a cozy atmosphere while maintaining privacy.
